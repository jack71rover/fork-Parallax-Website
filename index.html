Parallax Scrolling Effect

Author: Vineet Kumar

What is Parallax Scrolling?

Parallax scrolling is a web design technique where the background moves slower than the foreground. As visitors scroll down the page, it creates a 3D-like sense of depth, making the browsing experience more immersive. This effect originates from a visual illusion: the human eye perceives nearby objects as larger and distant objects as smaller and moving slower.

This concept was first used in traditional animation and early video games, such as Disney's "Snow White" and "Super Mario." With the evolution of CSS and HTML, the parallax effect gradually developed into a common web design technique seen today.

In 2007, developer Glutnix shared an early browser implementation and sample code on a blog. It wasn't until HTML5 and CSS3 became popular in 2011 that this technology rapidly gained popularity among designers. Proponents believe that parallax backgrounds can more effectively attract users and enhance the overall website experience; however, research also suggests that while parallax can enhance certain aspects of the experience, it does not necessarily mean the overall user experience is universally improved.


Why Use Parallax Images?

    There are two main reasons designers might try parallax images:

  • Better Storytelling

    Parallax images are particularly suitable for narrative storytelling. Good design focuses on conveying information: we hope to communicate with visitors through the website and tell the story of a product, service, or brand. Parallax scrolling can immerse users in a unique browsing experience, even allowing a complete brand story to be presented on a single-page website without needing to jump to multiple pages.

  • Increase Interactive Engagement

    The parallax effect can promote user interaction and help reduce the bounce rate. The bounce rate indicates the percentage of visitors who leave after viewing only a single page; a high bounce rate is detrimental to search rankings. A carefully crafted parallax page can provide a dynamic and engaging experience, encouraging visitors to stay longer.


Tips to keep in mind when adding parallax scrolling to your website:

  • Use Moderately

    Using the parallax effect in specific sections is better than using it throughout the entire site. It can be used in the header or title area, or limited to the homepage. The goal is to enhance the design, not distract attention.

  • Pay Attention to Image Compression

    The parallax effect often uses multiple media and graphics to create a sense of depth, but overly large images can slow down loading and affect the experience, so compression and optimization should be done well.

  • Make Good Use of Color

    Parallax is not just about the movement of the foreground and background; color can also add layers. Softer tones can be used for the background, while brighter colors are used for the foreground to enhance depth and focus.
